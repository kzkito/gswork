About us

We, Tokyo Dialysis Center, specialize in simplifying dialysis appointment reservations for travelers exploring Japan.

Our dedicated team works with top-quality dialysis facilities across the country to secure convenient appointments that align with your travel plans, leaving you free to savor Japan's beauty and culture.

Report Service

We will provide a list of several available hospitals in the area where you plan to stay, attaching a brief report. The report will include interior photos of the hospital, a their price list of dialysis and various options, and instructions on how to access the cite from major train stations. If you are going to stay in a major city, we can typically find from at least five hospitals. However, if you are in a suburban area, there may be only one selected option. If we cannot pick one hospital, we will refund the fee.

Reserve Service

The reserve service includes reserving appointment of sessions at a single hospital. For example, if you plan to undergo dialysis three times at the same hospital, please purchase one reserve service. If you plan to have two sessions at one hospital and other sessions at another hospital, please purchase two reserve services.

FAQ

How much does a single dialysis session cost?

Overall, it depends on the hospital. It's typically between JPY40,000 to 50,000, roughly around USD300.

Is dialysis safe in Japan?

Yes, it is very safe. According to a 2012 report, when Japan is considered as 1 for the risk of mortality among chronic dialysis patients, Europe reports a rate of 2.4 and the United States reports a rate of 2.81). Additionally, the incidence of all complications is much lower compared to other countries2).

1)Robinson BM, Bieber B, Pisoni RL, et al: Dialvsis Outcomes and Practice Patterns Study (DOPPS): its strengths, limitations, and role in informing practices and policies. Clin I Am Soc Nephrol 7: 1897-905, 2012

2)Robinson BM, Akizawa I, Jager KJ, et al: Factors affecting outcomes in patients reaching end-stage kidney disease worldwide: differences in access to renal replacement therapy, modality use, and haemodialysis practices. Lancet 388: 294-306, 2016

I would love to use your service! What do I need to prepare?

If you are using the Report service, please let me know the city where you plan to stay. For example, if it's Tokyo, we would need to know the specific areas like Asakusa or Shibuya. If you have the address of your intended hotel, please provide that as well!

For the Reserve service, it varies by hospital, but generally, we will need your recent dialysis records, any comorbidities other than kidney disease, other necessary dialysis-related values, and comments from your primary physician. Each hospital may have specific forms to fill out, so please reach out to us initially for further details.
